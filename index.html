About Me

Some people see data as numbers in rows and columns. I see stories. Patterns hiding in plain sight. Tiny signals whispering insights about human behavior, decisions, and change. That’s what pulled me toward data—and kept me there.

I'm Yash, someone who started in computer science but found purpose in the artistry of analytics. I thrive in the space where logic meets intuition. I’ve spent late nights scripting in Python, wrestling with messy spreadsheets, and crafting dashboards that don’t just report numbers—they guide decisions.

Tools like Power BI, SQL, and Excel are my brushstrokes. With them, I shape narratives from datasets, giving shape to the abstract and clarity to the complex. My work isn’t just about getting answers—it’s about asking better questions. If there’s one thing I’ve learned, it’s that the magic doesn’t lie in the data. It lies in what we choose to see through it.
